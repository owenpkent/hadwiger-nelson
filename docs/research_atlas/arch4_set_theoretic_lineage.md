# Architecture 4 dossier: set-theoretic / axiomatic structure of $\chi(\mathbb{R}^2)$ and its definable refinements

**Sub-corpus**: the dependence of the chromatic number of the unit-distance graph on $\mathbb{R}^2$ on the axioms of set theory; the descriptive-set-theoretic hierarchy $\chi \leq \chi_B \leq \chi_m$; effective / computable / projective refinements; the Shelah-Soifer 2003 phenomenon and its sequels (Shelah-Soifer 2003b, Payne 2009); recent (2018-2026) Borel-combinatorics work that touches the Hadwiger-Nelson setting.

**Scope**: Architecture 4 in the four-architecture frame. Combinatorial / SAT lower bounds (Arch 1), Falconer-style measurable lower bounds (Arch 2), LP / SDP / fractional bounds (Arch 3) appear only when they touch the axiomatic story. The cross-architecture coupling synthesized in `docs/03_research/cross_architecture_coupling.md` (L4) is referenced in section 5.

**Methodology caveat**: the three primary papers in this lineage (Shelah-Soifer 2003 JCT-A 103, Shelah-Soifer 2003 JCT-A 105, Payne 2009 EJC 16) all returned as binary PDFs on direct WebFetch and could not be parsed for full lemma-level text. I have read the abstracts, the Wikipedia / Soifer-2009 / *Mathematical Coloring Book* Chapter 47 secondary expositions, and Polymath16 wiki discussions. Where I am working from secondary sources I flag this explicitly. Two further primary sources (Kechris-Solecki-Todorcevic 1999 *Borel chromatic numbers*; Conley-Marks-Tucker-Drob 2016 "Brooks's theorem for measurable colorings") were fetched in abstract form only. I have not located any paper that proves a Borel-chromatic-number lower bound specific to the Euclidean unit-distance graph on $\mathbb{R}^2$ stronger than $\chi_B \geq \chi_m \geq 5$; flag this as a possible gap or as an unexplored question.

**Headline up front**: Architecture 4 is conceptually rich but corpus-small. It does **not** close the $5 \leq \chi(\mathbb{R}^2) \leq 7$ gap. Its role is to reframe what "the" chromatic number means by exhibiting graphs whose chromatic number depends on whether full AC, Lebesgue measurability (LM), or Borel definability is in force. For $\chi(\mathbb{R}^2)$ specifically, Shelah-Soifer 2003 is a *conditional* theorem: **if** every finite UDG in $\mathbb{R}^2$ has $\chi \leq 4$, **then** the chromatic number of the plane is $4$ in ZFC and at least $5$ in ZF + DC + LM. The de Grey 2018 lower bound ($\chi(\mathbb{R}^2) \geq 5$) rendered the hypothesis of the conditional false, so the conditional itself is now vacuous as a statement about $\chi(\mathbb{R}^2)$. The *phenomenon* (axiom-dependence of chromatic numbers of unit-distance-type graphs) survives the de Grey result; the precise plane-theoretic statement is now folkloric and waiting for a new conditional anchored at the current bounds.

## 1. The Shelah-Soifer phenomenon

### 1.1 The 2003 paper (JCT-A 103)

S. Shelah and A. Soifer, *Axiom of choice and chromatic number of the plane*, J. Combin. Theory A 103 (2003), 387-391. ([Shelah preprint E33](https://shelah.logic.at/files/95439/E33.pdf))

The headline construction is **not** the unit-distance graph on $\mathbb{R}^2$. It is an auxiliary distance graph $H$ on $\mathbb{R}$ (or, in the sequel, on $\mathbb{R}^2$) built so that its chromatic number provably differs between ZFC and ZF + DC + LM. The construction takes a Hamel-basis-style choice function in ZFC to produce a $2$-coloring; the same graph in ZF + DC + LM admits no countable measurable proper coloring because every coloring class would have positive density on an arithmetic-progression-like set and would force a monochromatic edge by Steinhaus's theorem.

**Theorem (Shelah-Soifer 2003, paraphrased from the Wikipedia / Soifer-2009 expositions)**. There is a graph $G$ on the real line with edges $\{x, y\} \subset \mathbb{R}$ defined by membership of $x - y$ in a specific countable set $S \subset \mathbb{R}$, such that $\chi(G) = 2$ in ZFC, and $\chi(G) \geq \aleph_0$ (uncountable in the original phrasing, "not countable" in some retellings) in ZF + DC + LM.

The set $S$ is chosen so that the additive subgroup of $\mathbb{R}$ generated by $S$ has structure that, under LM, forces any color class with positive density to contain a difference in $S$. This is the classical Steinhaus mechanism: a measurable set of positive density has its difference set containing an interval around $0$. Under AC, one selects coset representatives of an appropriate quotient and 2-colors by parity of a Hamel coordinate; under LM no such selection is available.

**Conditional chromatic theorem**. Shelah-Soifer 2003 also state a *conditional* theorem about $\chi(\mathbb{R}^2)$ itself: **if** every finite unit-distance subgraph of $\mathbb{R}^2$ has chromatic number at most $4$, **then**

- $\chi(\mathbb{R}^2) = 4$ in ZFC (by the de Bruijn-Erdős compactness theorem, which itself uses a weak form of AC);
- $\chi(\mathbb{R}^2) \geq 5$ in ZF + DC + LM (because the measurable-coloring barrier forces at least Falconer's bound).

So under this hypothesis, $\chi(\mathbb{R}^2)$ would *literally* be different in the two axiom systems. The hypothesis was open at the time. **de Grey 2018 falsified the hypothesis**: there exists a finite UDG ($1581$ vertices, later $509$) with $\chi \geq 5$. The conditional is now vacuously true and its consequent unprovable from its hypothesis. The Shelah-Soifer mechanism remains a valid demonstration that *some* unit-distance-type graphs have axiom-dependent chromatic numbers; whether the unit-distance graph on $\mathbb{R}^2$ itself does is again open.

### 1.2 The 2003 sequel (JCT-A 105)

S. Shelah and A. Soifer, *Axiom of choice and chromatic number: examples on the plane*, J. Combin. Theory A 105 (2003). ([Shelah preprint E51](https://shelah.logic.at/files/95685/E51.pdf))

Where the first paper exhibits the phenomenon on $\mathbb{R}^1$ and gives the conditional on $\mathbb{R}^2$, the sequel constructs an explicit *planar* distance graph $G_2$ with the axiom-dependent chromatic number. Secondary sources (Boronska thesis, Soifer 2009 Chapter 47) report: $\chi(G_2) = 4$ in ZFC, and $\chi(G_2)$ is not countable in ZF + DC + LM. The graph $G_2$ is **not** the unit-distance graph on $\mathbb{R}^2$. It is a distance graph for a carefully chosen countable distance set, embedded in the plane, designed so that the Hamel-basis 4-coloring exists under AC and is destroyed by measurability.

### 1.3 The Shelah-Soifer 2003c paper on $\mathbb{R}^n$

S. Shelah and A. Soifer, *Axiom of choice and chromatic number of $\mathbb{R}^n$*, J. Combin. Theory A 110 (2005). Extends the 2003 construction to higher dimensions with refined parameters. Same mechanism; not specific to $\mathbb{R}^2$.

### 1.4 Payne 2009

M. S. Payne, *Unit distance graphs with ambiguous chromatic number*, Electronic J. Combinatorics 16 (2009), Note 31. ([arXiv:0707.1177](https://arxiv.org/abs/0707.1177))

**Key advance**: Payne constructs *unit-distance* graphs in $\mathbb{R}^n$ (i.e., subgraphs of the actual Hadwiger-Nelson graph) with axiom-dependent chromatic number. The Shelah-Soifer 2003 examples use a *general* distance set, not the single distance $1$. Payne's construction is the first explicit family of UDG-subgraphs with chromatic number depending on AC vs LM, narrowing the gap between the abstract phenomenon and the actual Hadwiger-Nelson setting.

The construction: take a Hamel basis of $\mathbb{R}$ over $\mathbb{Q}$, define a graph on a carefully chosen subset of $\mathbb{R}^n$ where edges correspond to differences whose Hamel coordinates lie in a specific countable set, and verify that all such edges have Euclidean length exactly $1$. Under AC, the basis exists, and a finite proper coloring exists with chromatic number $\chi_{ZFC} = c$ for some explicit small $c$ depending on the construction parameters. Under LM, no measurable Hamel basis exists and the same coloring construction fails, forcing $\chi_{LM} = \aleph_0$ or larger. Payne does not give explicit numerical values for $\chi$ in the abstract that I could verify against the full PDF, but the abstract emphasizes that the construction provides "further evidence that the chromatic number of the plane might depend on set theory."

### 1.5 What this means for $\chi(\mathbb{R}^2)$ specifically vs an artificial graph

This is the load-bearing structural distinction:

- **Shelah-Soifer 2003 (J.C.T.A. 103, 105, 110) construct artificial graphs**: not the unit-distance graph on $\mathbb{R}^2$, but distance graphs where the distance set is chosen to exhibit axiom-dependence. They are subgraphs of $\mathbb{R}^2 \times \mathbb{R}^2$, but the relation "$\|x - y\| = 1$" is replaced by "$x - y \in S$" for a specifically chosen countable $S$. **The Hadwiger-Nelson graph itself is not the example.**
- **Payne 2009 constructs unit-distance subgraphs**: this is much closer to the canonical $\chi(\mathbb{R}^2)$ problem because every edge is now genuinely a unit-distance edge. But the *coloring* whose chromatic-number-depends-on-AC behavior is exhibited is on the specific Payne subgraph, not on all of $\mathbb{R}^2$.
- **The unit-distance graph $G_{\mathbb{R}^2}$ itself**: it is **not known** whether $\chi(G_{\mathbb{R}^2})$ depends on AC. Soifer conjectures it might; nobody has proved it does (or that it does not). The phenomenon is *consistent* with the structure of $\mathbb{R}^2$ but has not been pinned to the unit-distance edge set specifically. This is the gap the architecture has not closed and may never close.

## 2. The hierarchy $\chi \leq \chi_B \leq \chi_m$

### 2.1 Definitions

For a graph $G$ on a Polish space $X$ with Borel edge set:

- $\chi(G)$: smallest $k$ such that some $c: X \to \{1, \ldots, k\}$ is a proper coloring. No definability constraint on $c$. Existence of $c$ for finite $k$ uses AC or the de Bruijn-Erdős compactness theorem.
- $\chi_B(G)$ (**Borel chromatic**): smallest $k$ such that the color classes $c^{-1}(i)$ are all Borel sets. Equivalently, $c: X \to \{1, \ldots, k\}$ is Borel-measurable for the discrete sigma-algebra on the target. ([Kechris-Solecki-Todorcevic 1999](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/BOREL-CHROMATIC-NUMBERS-Kechris-Solecki/b60dc62a89528ccd4181c9cf0836154c055c0ede))
- $\chi_m(G)$ (**Lebesgue-measurable chromatic**): smallest $k$ such that the color classes are Lebesgue-measurable. ([Falconer 1981, J. Combin. Theory A 31, 184-189](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hadwiger%E2%80%93Nelson_problem))
- $\chi_\Delta(G)$ (**$\Delta$-chromatic**): variants for higher levels of the descriptive hierarchy, e.g., color classes in $\Sigma^1_1$, $\Pi^1_1$, $\Delta^1_2$, etc. These appear in the projective hierarchy and are studied by Marks and collaborators.

Borel sets are strictly contained in Lebesgue-measurable sets, so $\chi_B(G) \geq \chi_m(G)$ for the same graph. Both depend on the ambient $\sigma$-algebra and so depend on the axiom system (under ZF + DC + LM, every set is Lebesgue measurable, so $\chi_m = \chi$; under ZFC, $\chi_m$ can be strictly larger than $\chi$ because the AC-witness $c$ need not be measurable).

The chain is:

$$
\chi(G_{\mathbb{R}^2}) \;\leq\; \chi_B(G_{\mathbb{R}^2}) \;\leq\; \chi_m(G_{\mathbb{R}^2}).
$$

(I write this in the direction "more definability constraint $\Rightarrow$ larger or equal chromatic number" because adding constraints to the coloring can only increase the minimum number of colors.)

### 2.2 What is known about strictness

- **$\chi \leq \chi_B$**: open whether strict for the canonical Euclidean unit-distance graph. No published example of $\chi_B(G_{\mathbb{R}^2}) > \chi(G_{\mathbb{R}^2})$. Note that under ZFC the AC-witness $c: \mathbb{R}^2 \to \{1, \ldots, k\}$ may not be Borel even if a Borel proper coloring exists; the inequality is about *existence* of a Borel coloring.
- **$\chi_B \leq \chi_m$**: open whether strict for $G_{\mathbb{R}^2}$. Borel sets are strictly contained in measurable, but a measurable coloring need not be Borelizable.
- **$\chi_m$ vs $\chi$ in ZFC**: open. The Shelah-Soifer phenomenon shows the gap can be the difference between $4$ and $\aleph_0$ in *some* graphs. For $G_{\mathbb{R}^2}$ itself, current bounds (Arch 1, Arch 2) give $5 \leq \chi \leq 7$ and $5 \leq \chi_m \leq 7$ with no published strict separation.
- **$\chi_m = \chi$ in ZF + DC + LM**: trivially yes, because every coloring is measurable in that model.

### 2.3 What the cross-architecture coupling (L4) implies for the hierarchy

LEARNINGS L4 (`docs/03_research/cross_architecture_coupling.md` §L4) observed that pushing Falconer's $\chi_m \geq 5$ to $\chi_m \geq 6$ requires *the same missing object* as pushing de Grey's $\chi \geq 5$ to $\chi \geq 6$: a finite 6-chromatic UDG in $\mathbb{R}^2$. This has a clean Architecture-4 reading: *if* such a UDG were found, it would lift through the entire hierarchy. A finite 6-chromatic UDG is a Borel object (trivially: it is finite, hence has discrete topology, hence every subset is Borel and Lebesgue-measurable in the ambient $\mathbb{R}^2$). So the existence of a finite 6-chromatic UDG would force $\chi \geq 6$, $\chi_B \geq 6$, **and** $\chi_m \geq 6$ simultaneously, by de Bruijn-Erdős and the standard density-extraction (Falconer) argument.

This means the hierarchy is **degenerate at the level of integer-quantization barriers**: the obstructions are not at the $\chi$-vs-$\chi_B$ or $\chi_B$-vs-$\chi_m$ boundary, they are at the joint barrier of "no finite 6-chromatic UDG known." Architecture 4 cannot peel apart the layers as long as Architecture 1 has not produced the witness.

Conversely, if no finite 6-chromatic UDG exists (i.e., if $\chi(\mathbb{R}^2) = 5$), then by the compactness theorem $\chi(G_{\mathbb{R}^2}) = 5$ in ZFC, and the question becomes whether $\chi_m, \chi_B > 5$ is possible. This would be an Architecture-4-and-Architecture-2-coupled question: a measurable-coloring obstruction that has no finite combinatorial witness. The cleanest analog is the Shelah-Soifer 2003 distance graph on $\mathbb{R}^1$, which has $\chi = 2$ in ZFC (trivial) and $\chi_m = \aleph_0$. A planar analog at the level of $\chi = 5$ vs $\chi_m \geq 6$ would be a real Architecture-4 result; it has not been produced.

## 4. What this architecture can and cannot do

### 4.1 Can do

- **Reframe what "the" chromatic number means.** Architecture 4 has firmly established that *some* geometric coloring problems are axiom-dependent. This is structurally important even though it does not pin down $\chi(G_{\mathbb{R}^2})$.
- **Force the Borel / measurable distinction onto the agenda.** The hierarchy $\chi \leq \chi_B \leq \chi_m$ is a genuine refinement of the Hadwiger-Nelson question. Each level is a different mathematical object and admits different methods.
- **Provide consistency results.** Shelah-Soifer 2003-2005 prove that statements like "$\chi(G) = 4$" and "$\chi(G) = \aleph_0$" are simultaneously consistent with weakly-related axiom systems for explicit $G$. This kills naive intuitions that the chromatic number is a "robust" geometric invariant.
- **Connect to descriptive-set-theoretic technology.** Borel-chromatic-number machinery (Kechris-Solecki-Todorcevic 1999; the $G_0$ dichotomy; the Marks measurable-graph-combinatorics framework) is a sharp tool that *could* produce new lower bounds on $\chi_B(G_{\mathbb{R}^2})$.

### 4.2 Cannot do (honestly)

- **Close the $5 \leq \chi(\mathbb{R}^2) \leq 7$ gap directly.** The Shelah-Soifer mechanism produces chromatic-number-jumps in graphs *designed* to exhibit them. The unit-distance graph $G_{\mathbb{R}^2}$ is rigid (every edge is at Euclidean distance exactly $1$, no design freedom). Whether the rigidity destroys the axiom-dependent phenomenon, or whether the phenomenon survives in some hidden form, is open. As of 2026 there is no published Architecture-4 lower-bound on $\chi(G_{\mathbb{R}^2})$ stronger than the Arch-1 / Arch-2 bound of $5$.
- **Decide whether $\chi(G_{\mathbb{R}^2})$ itself depends on axioms.** Soifer's conjecture is that it does. There is no proof and no counter-proof.
- **Lift the cross-architecture coupling (L4).** The missing 6-chromatic finite UDG is a Borel object; if it exists, Architecture 4 inherits the breakthrough but does not produce it. Architecture 4 is *downstream* of Architecture 1 on the integer-quantization side.

### 4.3 The honest assessment

Architecture 4 is **structurally illuminating** and **operationally orthogonal** to the bound question. It explains *why* the four-architecture framing is sensible (the Borel / measurable / general chromatic numbers are different objects) and *what would be true* if various conditional hypotheses held. It does not move the bound. The right tone for the project is: include Architecture 4 in the atlas, document the Shelah-Soifer / Payne / Schmerl lineage carefully, but do not stake bound-progress on it.

## 5. Cross-architecture connection (L4 lens)

LEARNINGS L4 said: the obstruction to extending Falconer's $\chi_m \geq 5$ to $\chi_m \geq 6$ is the same as the obstruction to extending de Grey's $\chi \geq 5$ to $\chi \geq 6$: the missing finite 6-chromatic UDG.

How does Architecture 4 sit relative to this?

**Three modes**.

**Mode A (downstream)**: if Architecture 1 finds a finite 6-chromatic UDG, Architecture 4 inherits $\chi \geq 6$, $\chi_B \geq 6$, $\chi_m \geq 6$ for free (the witness is a finite Borel set; all three chromatic numbers lift). Architecture 4 contributes nothing new in this mode.

**Mode B (orthogonal)**: there is one *independent* attack route Architecture 4 controls: prove that *no* Borel proper $5$-coloring of $G_{\mathbb{R}^2}$ exists. This would give $\chi_B(G_{\mathbb{R}^2}) \geq 6$ even if Architecture 1 cannot exhibit a finite 6-chromatic UDG (because Borel obstructions need not have finite witnesses). Such a result would *not* automatically imply $\chi(G_{\mathbb{R}^2}) \geq 6$ in ZFC, because the AC-witness to a 5-coloring can be non-Borel. But it would be a real refinement of the bound on $\chi_B$ that is not available to Architectures 1, 2, 3. The technology to attempt this is the Kechris-Solecki-Todorcevic / Marks-Conley-Tucker-Drob descriptive-set-theoretic machinery. As of 2026, no such result is published. **This is the natural Architecture-4 attack point.**

**Mode C (meta-mathematical)**: prove that "$\chi(G_{\mathbb{R}^2}) = k$" is independent of ZFC for some $k \in \{5, 6, 7\}$. This would be a Shelah-Soifer-style result *specific to the unit-distance graph*, not to an artificial graph. The known examples (Shelah-Soifer 2003, Payne 2009) come close but do not target the canonical UDG. If achieved, it would resolve the long-standing folklore question "does $\chi(G_{\mathbb{R}^2})$ depend on AC?" affirmatively. This is the highest-payoff Architecture-4 outcome and the most speculative.

**Honest summary**: Architecture 4 sits **mostly orthogonal** to L4. It does not *contribute* to closing the bound on $\chi$, but it offers two independent refinements ($\chi_B$ bounds via descriptive set theory; ZFC-independence results) that no other architecture can produce. The independent value is real but limited.

## 6. What is tractable computationally / formally

Architecture 4 is the least computational of the four architectures. SAT solvers do not help (the constructions are infinite by design). LP / SDP does not help (the issue is definability, not optimization). The natural tools are:

- **Formal verification (Lean / Mathlib)**: prove existence / non-existence results about specific finite or countable graphs in a fixed axiom system.
- **Hand-checked descriptive-set-theoretic constructions**: verify the Shelah-Soifer construction at the lemma level, identify what would be needed to transport it to $G_{\mathbb{R}^2}$.

### Candidate experiments

**E4.1 (Lean): formalize the Shelah-Soifer 2003 toy example on $\mathbb{R}^1$.** The simplest version of the phenomenon is a distance graph on $\mathbb{R}$ with $\chi = 2$ in ZFC and $\chi$ uncountable in ZF + DC + LM. Mathlib's `MeasureTheory.Set.Lebesgue` and `LinearAlgebra.Basis` are sufficient to define both witnesses. The key lemmas are (a) under AC, a Hamel basis exists; (b) under LM, no Hamel basis is measurable (this is the classical Sierpinski-Vitali content). A clean Lean target: define a graph `ShelahSoifer.G : SimpleGraph ℝ`, prove `chromaticNumber G ≤ 2` using a Hamel-basis selection, and state (but not prove, because LM is not available in Mathlib's foundations) the LM-version as a theorem schema. **Realistic scope**: 2-4 weeks of Lean work, with the LM side being an unprovable-in-Mathlib axiom schema for now. Aligns with the existing Lean substrate (`HadwigerNelson.MoserBridge`) in that it gives a different *kind* of chromatic-number theorem.

**E4.2 (Lean): formalize Woodall 1973 ($\chi(\mathbb{Q}^2) = 2$).** This is HN-5 in the existing Lean roadmap (per session notes). Woodall's proof is: classify $(x, y) \in \mathbb{Q}^2$ by the parity of the sum of denominators of $x$ and $y$ (in lowest terms). The argument is short and Mathlib already has rational-arithmetic infrastructure. **Realistic scope**: 1-2 weeks. Output: `HadwigerNelson.Controls.Rational.chromatic_Q2_eq_2`. This is an Architecture-4-flavored result because it is the canonical wrong-approach-detector for Architecture 1 (any combinatorial method that lifts to $\mathbb{Q}^2$ would force $\chi(\mathbb{Q}^2) \geq 3$ and contradict Woodall).

**E4.3 (expository): formalize / document the Borel chromatic number hierarchy.** Write a careful exposition (Markdown, not Lean) of the chain $\chi \leq \chi_B \leq \chi_m \leq \chi_{\Sigma^1_1} \leq \ldots$ and what is known / unknown for $G_{\mathbb{R}^2}$ at each level. This is **expository**, not experimental, but the project lacks this material and Soifer 2009 Chapter 47 does not cover the Borel side at depth. **Realistic scope**: 1 week of writing. Output: `docs/03_research/borel_chromatic_hierarchy.md`.

**E4.4 (very speculative): re-examine the Shelah-Soifer conditional after de Grey 2018.** The 2003 conditional ("if all finite UDGs in $\mathbb{R}^2$ have $\chi \leq 4$ then ...") is now vacuous because de Grey found a 5-chromatic UDG. Is there a *new* conditional with the current bound? Specifically: "if all finite UDGs in $\mathbb{R}^2$ have $\chi \leq 5$ then $\chi(G_{\mathbb{R}^2}) = 5$ in ZFC and $\chi(G_{\mathbb{R}^2}) \geq 6$ in ZF + DC + LM." This requires the Shelah-Soifer mechanism to be re-tuned at the new threshold. I am not aware of any published attempt. **Realistic scope**: unbounded; this would be a real research result. List it as an **open problem** in the dossier, not as a tractable experiment.

E4.1 and E4.2 are concrete and worth pursuing if Architecture 4 receives any project investment. E4.3 is the lowest-cost high-value item (a survey document, no formal work). E4.4 is the project's most speculative Architecture-4 direction and should be tagged as such.

## 7. Wrong-approach-detector check

Architecture 4 sits partly outside the project's detector framework (`$\mathbb{Q}^2$, $L^\infty$, $\mathbb{R}^1$`) because the question it asks (axiom-dependence) is meta-mathematical. But the detectors still engage in two ways.

**Detector 1: $\mathbb{Q}^2$ ($\chi = 2$, Woodall 1973).** Does a method for "axiom-dependent chromatic number on $\mathbb{R}^2$" also force axiom-dependence on $\mathbb{Q}^2$?

- **Shelah-Soifer 2003 mechanism**: the construction uses a Hamel basis of $\mathbb{R}$ over $\mathbb{Q}$. On $\mathbb{Q}^2$ the field is $\mathbb{Q}$ itself, the "Hamel basis" is trivial (just $\{1\}$), and the mechanism degenerates. No axiom-dependence is forced on $\mathbb{Q}^2$. Woodall's $\chi(\mathbb{Q}^2) = 2$ is unaffected. **Detector passes**: the Shelah-Soifer machinery uses the topology and density of $\mathbb{R}$ (specifically the existence of a Hamel basis over $\mathbb{Q}$, which requires $\mathbb{R} \setminus \mathbb{Q}$ to be uncountable) in an essential way.
- **Payne 2009 unit-distance variant**: same Hamel-basis ingredient. **Detector passes** for the same reason.

**Detector 2: $L^\infty$ on $\mathbb{R}^2$ ($\chi = 4$, Chilakamarri).** Does the axiom-dependence mechanism use the Euclidean rigidity (equilateral triangles, regular pentagons) of $\mathbb{R}^2$?

- The Shelah-Soifer / Payne constructions use a *general* distance set or unit-distance subgraphs with Hamel-basis-tuned vertex sets. The constructions do not depend on the Euclidean unit circle being smooth (regular pentagons live in $\mathbb{R}^2$ but not in $L^\infty$). The mechanism would *survive* a change of norm, but the *finite-graph 4-coloring witness* (the ZFC side) is norm-dependent. The construction therefore engages with the norm structure indirectly through the witness, not through the axiom-dependence proof. **Detector engagement is mixed**: the LM-side argument is norm-blind; the AC-side witness is norm-sensitive. Flag this as a partial detector engagement.

**Detector 3: $\mathbb{R}^1$ ($\chi = 2$).** The Shelah-Soifer 2003 mechanism is *demonstrated* on $\mathbb{R}^1$ in the original paper (the line graph $H$). The line has $\chi(H) = 2$ in ZFC and $\chi(H) = \aleph_0$ in ZF + DC + LM. **Detector engages but the conclusion is the opposite of what the detector usually checks**: rather than catching a method that *overshoots* to $\mathbb{R}^1$, the detector here observes that axiom-dependence on $\mathbb{R}^1$ is the *intended* phenomenon. The mechanism is not overshooting; it is doing its job. The detector is not violated, but it does not provide its usual structural sanity check.

**Overall verdict**: Architecture 4 mechanisms pass the $\mathbb{Q}^2$ detector cleanly (Hamel basis ingredient breaks on $\mathbb{Q}^2$). They engage the $L^\infty$ detector only partially (AC-side witnesses use Euclidean rigidity; LM-side proofs do not). They are at-best-orthogonal to the $\mathbb{R}^1$ detector because $\mathbb{R}^1$ is the test bed for the phenomenon, not a degenerate case to be ruled out.

## 8. What this enables / what remains open

**Enables (downstream agents)**:

- **BUILDER**: the highest-leverage Architecture-4 BUILDER target is **E4.4 (re-tune the Shelah-Soifer conditional to the post-de-Grey threshold)**. If it can be done, it gives a clean conditional "either $\chi(\mathbb{R}^2) \geq 6$ (Arch 1 breakthrough), or there is an AC-dependent gap between $\chi$ and $\chi_m$ that is genuinely a foundations-of-set-theory phenomenon." This is a substantial research result and well outside the project's current scope; flag as long-term. **E4.1 (Lean formalization of the toy Shelah-Soifer example on $\mathbb{R}^1$)** is a tractable BUILDER task: it teaches the project's Lean infrastructure how to express axiom-dependence statements and is reusable. **E4.2 (Lean Woodall)** is the Architecture-4 wrong-approach-detector formalization and dovetails with the existing Lean substrate.

- **VERIFIER**: any claimed result of the form "$\chi(\mathbb{R}^2) \geq k$ in ZF + DC + LM" must be checked against the Shelah-Soifer ladder: does the proof reduce to (a) a Hamel-basis argument that breaks under LM, (b) a Falconer-style Lebesgue-density argument, or (c) something genuinely new? Categories (a) and (b) are inherited from Arch 4 and Arch 2; category (c) is novel and warrants the most scrutiny.

- **ADVERSARY**: the strongest adversarial question for Architecture 4 is "**does the rigidity of the unit-distance graph destroy the Shelah-Soifer phenomenon?**" The Hamel-basis construction has design freedom; the actual unit-distance graph does not. It is possible (and Soifer conjectures the opposite) that the unit-distance graph is *too rigid* to admit an axiom-dependent chromatic number. If true, this would be a *negative* Architecture-4 result and would settle one piece of the foundational landscape. The adversary should look for an argument that AC and LM agree on $\chi(G_{\mathbb{R}^2})$ specifically.

- **SYNTHESIZER**: the Borel / measurable / general chromatic hierarchy ($\chi \leq \chi_B \leq \chi_m$) should be added to the atlas's variants table with the honest note that strictness is unknown for every adjacent pair in the canonical Euclidean setting.

**Remains open**:

- Does $\chi(G_{\mathbb{R}^2})$ depend on AC? Open since Shelah-Soifer 2003 and not directly addressed by de Grey 2018.
- Is $\chi_B(G_{\mathbb{R}^2}) > \chi_m(G_{\mathbb{R}^2})$? Open. No published example of strict inequality on the canonical graph.
- Is $\chi_m(G_{\mathbb{R}^2}) > \chi(G_{\mathbb{R}^2})$ in ZFC? Open. The Shelah-Soifer phenomenon exhibits this for artificial graphs, not for $G_{\mathbb{R}^2}$.
- Can the Marks / Conley / Tucker-Drob descriptive-graph-combinatorics machinery (the "$G_0$ dichotomy", Brooks for measurable colorings, etc.) be applied to $G_{\mathbb{R}^2}$ specifically? Open. The framework exists and the unit-distance graph is a natural test case; I have not found a published attempt.
- Is there a new conditional of the form "if all finite UDGs in $\mathbb{R}^2$ have $\chi \leq 5$, then $\chi(G_{\mathbb{R}^2}) = 5$ in ZFC and $\chi(G_{\mathbb{R}^2}) \geq 6$ in ZF + DC + LM"? Open and worth attempting.

**Flag for follow-up SURVEYOR sessions**:

- (a) **Primary text of Shelah-Soifer 2003 (J.C.T.A. 103 and 105) and Payne 2009**: PDFs were not directly parseable. A targeted session to extract the exact constructions at the lemma level would let the dossier upgrade the section 1 paraphrases to verified statements.
- (b) **Kechris-Solecki-Todorcevic 1999 and Kechris-Marks survey on descriptive graph combinatorics**: the foundational framework for $\chi_B$. A focused dossier on what it gives for *geometric* graphs (as opposed to shift graphs and equivalence relations, which are the canonical examples) would establish what Architecture 4 can technically deliver.
- (c) **Recent (2020-2025) Marks-style results**: I located several relevant 2020-2024 papers (Conley-Marks-Tucker-Drob 2016, *Hyperfiniteness and Borel combinatorics*; the 2025 Borel polychromatic number paper) but not a paper that targets $G_{\mathbb{R}^2}$. A targeted literature search asking "has anyone computed $\chi_B(G_{\mathbb{R}^2})$ explicitly?" would close the gap.
- (d) **Soifer 2009 Chapter 47 and Soifer 2024 *New Mathematical Coloring Book* axiomatic chapters**: not directly accessible to me in this session. They are the canonical secondary exposition; a SURVEYOR session with physical-book access would verify the section 1-2 paraphrases.
