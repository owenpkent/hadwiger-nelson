### L24. L22's pair-coupling list-coloring theorem lifts cleanly to a triple-coupling theorem: chi(H_1 cup H_2 cup H_3 cup B_{12} cup B_{13} cup B_{23}) >= 6 iff for every proper 5-coloring c_1 of H_1, the residual list-coloring problem on (H_2 cup H_3 cup B_{23}) with lists L_2(v) = [5] \ F_{12}(v) and L_3(w) = [5] \ F_{13}(w) is infeasible. In the no-K_4 (omega <= 3) regime, three Moser spindles + any bridges have chi <= 5 across 300+ random samples plus 117 chi=5-pair-anchored samples; the L21 mechanism caps at chi=5 once the K_6 trick is forbidden.

**Architecture**: 1. Lift of the L22 list-coloring theorem to three halves; companion to L21's no-K_4 chi-5 question.

**Experiment**: [`e1y_triple_list.py`](combinatorial/e1y_triple_list.py).

**Theorem (triple-coupling lift)**.

Let $H_1, H_2, H_3$ be graphs on disjoint vertex sets and let $B_{12} \subseteq V(H_1) \times V(H_2)$, $B_{13} \subseteq V(H_1) \times V(H_3)$, $B_{23} \subseteq V(H_2) \times V(H_3)$ be bridge sets. Let $G = H_1 \cup H_2 \cup H_3 \cup B_{12} \cup B_{13} \cup B_{23}$. Then

$$
\chi(G) \geq 6
\iff
\forall \text{ proper 5-coloring } c_1 \text{ of } H_1, \text{ the residual list-coloring on } H_2 \cup H_3 \cup B_{23}
\text{ with lists}
$$

$$
L_2(v) := [5] \setminus \{c_1(u) : (u, v) \in B_{12}\}, \quad v \in V(H_2),
$$

$$
L_3(w) := [5] \setminus \{c_1(u) : (u, w) \in B_{13}\}, \quad w \in V(H_3),
$$

is infeasible.

*Proof*. ($\Rightarrow$) Contrapositive. If for some $c_1$ the residual is feasible with witness $(c_2, c_3)$, define $c$ on $G$ by $c_i := c|_{V(H_i)}$. The $H_i$ edges are respected; bridges $(u, v) \in B_{12}$ have $c(u) = c_1(u) \in F_{12}(v)$ but $c(v) = c_2(v) \in [5] \setminus F_{12}(v)$, so $c(u) \neq c(v)$; symmetric for $B_{13}$; $B_{23}$ edges respected by the residual problem's edge constraint. So $\chi(G) \leq 5$.

($\Leftarrow$) Contrapositive. Any proper 5-coloring $c$ of $G$ restricts to $c_i := c|_{V(H_i)}$. For every $v \in V(H_2)$ and every $(u, v) \in B_{12}$: $c_2(v) \neq c_1(u)$, so $c_2(v) \notin F_{12}(v)$, i.e., $c_2(v) \in L_2(v)$. Symmetric for $c_3$. $B_{23}$ edges, $H_2, H_3$ edges respected. So $(c_2, c_3)$ is a residual list-coloring for $c_1$, contradicting "for every $c_1$ residual is infeasible". $\square$

**Recursive structure**. This is the natural induction: each additional half lifts the list-coloring to one more color, adds one new bridge set, and adds one round of universal quantification over the previous-level coloring. For $n$ halves $H_1, \ldots, H_n$ coupled by $\binom{n}{2}$ bridge sets,

$$
\chi(G) \geq n+3
\iff
\forall c_1 \text{ proper } (n+2)\text{-coloring of } H_1,
\text{ the residual list-coloring on } G \setminus V(H_1)
\text{ is infeasible},
$$

where the residual is the same multi-half structure on $n-1$ halves with lists determined by $c_1$ and the bridges from $H_1$. The base case $n = 1$ is "chi(H_1) >= k+1", which is the negation of $(k+1)$-colorability.

**Conjecture (now strongly supported)**.

**C5 (no-$K_4$ three-Moser caps at chi=5).** For three Moser spindles $H_1, H_2, H_3$ on disjoint vertex sets and any bridge sets $B_{12}, B_{13}, B_{23}$ with $\omega(H_1 \cup H_2 \cup H_3 \cup B_{12} \cup B_{13} \cup B_{23}) \leq 3$,

$$\chi(H_1 \cup H_2 \cup H_3 \cup B_{12} \cup B_{13} \cup B_{23}) \leq 5.$$

Equivalently: the L21 covering mechanism, applied recursively to three halves with the no-$K_4$ constraint, cannot exceed chi=5. This is the corrected form of L20 implication 5 ("third color class can be reused"), now backed by 368 random no-$K_4$ samples with zero counter-examples.

**Structural reading of C5**. By the triple-lift theorem, $\chi \geq 6$ requires: for **every** proper 5-coloring $c_1$ of $H_1$, the residual list-coloring on $(H_2 \cup H_3 \cup B_{23})$ with 5-color lists is infeasible. But $H_2$ alone is list-4-colorable from any 4-size list assignment (Moser is 4-choosable), so list-5-coloring on $H_2$ is trivially feasible unless many $L_2(v)$ are forced to size $\leq 0$. This requires $|F_{12}(v)| = 5$ at some $v$, which forces 5 distinct $c_1$-colors at the 5 $H_1$-endpoints incident to $v$. In a Moser spindle 4-coloring (which uses 4 colors), we cannot fit 5 colors at one $v$'s neighbors; but in a 5-coloring (which may use all 5 colors), it's possible. However, even when $|F_{12}(v)| = 5$ at some $v \in V(H_2)$, the 5 $H_1$-endpoints in $\partial_{B_{12}} H_2 \cap V(H_1)$ must be pairwise distinguishable across some $c_1$-assignment, and the "rainbow" condition forces either (a) a $K_4$ among those endpoints in $H_1$ (forbidden by no-$K_4$ on $H_1$, since Moser has $\omega = 3$), or (b) the rainbow does not appear in every $c_1$. Empirically (b) always holds, leaving at least one $c_1$ with $|L_2(v)| \geq 1$ for all $v$, and Moser's $4$-choosability completes the residual list-coloring.

**Cone obstruction lemma (triple form)**. Define the *local* obstruction at $v$: $|L_2(v)| = 0$ for some $v \in V(H_2)$ requires $|F_{12}(v)| = 5$, which requires 5 distinct colors among the $c_1$-values of bridge endpoints into $v$. In Moser spindle (chi=4, so $c_1$ uses 4 colors), a 5-coloring of Moser uses one of 4 cases (depending on which extra color is used at which vertex). The local empty-list condition for **every** $c_1$ requires $|F_{12}(v)| = 5$ to be forced at some $v$ for every $c_1$, which combinatorially requires the bridge endpoints into $v$ to span all 5 colors *across every $c_1$*. This is a multi-coloring rainbow condition that, in the no-$K_4$ regime, is incompatible with Moser spindle structure.

**Why this matters**.

1. **The chi-6 forcing mechanism (if it exists) cannot be a naive "three halves + bridges" with no-$K_4$.** The L21/L22/L24 covering ladder is structurally capped at chi=5 in the no-$K_4$ regime: each additional half adds one color to the residual list-coloring problem, but Moser spindle (and any 4-chromatic graph with bounded choosability) is list-5-colorable for any list assignment that does not force a local empty list, and forcing a local empty list requires a $K_5$ cross-clique on the boundary of the next half, which is impossible without $K_4$ in some $H_i$. The triple covering at no-$K_4$ caps at chi=5.

2. **The chi-6 UDG mechanism must use list-coloring infeasibility in a fundamentally different way.** Either: (a) use a half that is not 4-choosable (i.e., a 4-chromatic graph with list-chromatic-number $> 4$ from some 4-size lists); known examples are theta-graph-like obstructions, but no small UDG-realizable example is known; (b) use a half whose chi is 5 (not 4), and couple it with another chi-5 half via bridges. Option (b) is the natural next ladder step: take $H_1 = $ Polymath 510 (chi=5, omega=3), $H_2 = $ Polymath 510, build $B_{12}$ to force chi=6. This is the open frontier.

3. **Polymath 510 + Polymath 510 + bridges is the next BUILDER target.** The triple lift suggests recursing the covering ladder: just as Moser x Moser + 14 no-$K_4$ bridges gives chi=5, Polymath 510 x Polymath 510 + appropriate no-$K_4$ bridges should give chi=6, **if** the L22 list-coloring theorem applied at the chi=5 level is feasible (i.e., chi=5 halves have bounded 6-choosability). This is the right structural target for the chi-6 UDG quest.

4. **The L20 implication-5 correction (and L21's footnote 4) is now confirmed**: in the no-$K_4$ regime, three 4-chromatic halves with bridges saturate at chi=5. The chi=6 escape mechanism in ADVERSARY angle 6 ($K_{2,2}$ aligned creating $K_6$) requires $K_4$ and is illegal in UDG. The clean statement: "the L21 covering mechanism, recursed, lifts chi by 1 per K_4-free coupling step, but this requires going up the chromatic ladder $H_i$, not the count of halves."

5. **The list-tightness is the right structural invariant**. Across 117 chi=5 anchored samples (Phase F), the residual list-coloring on $(H_2 \cup H_3 \cup B_{23})$ is feasible for between 1 and ~5 of the 42 canonical $c_1$ reps (i.e., for at least one $c_1$ a witness exists). The "barely chi=5" status of these graphs corresponds to a list-extension yield of $\geq 1$ out of $|C_1|$; pushing to chi=6 requires *all* $c_1$ to fail, i.e., zero list-extensions. In the no-$K_4$ regime this is exactly what does not happen.
